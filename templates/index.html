Rotary Organ Donation

Rotary Organ Donation is an independent online platform to further the cause of cadaver organ donation in India through educating the public about the importance of organ donation. Our aim is to educate the importance of organ donation and its approach to save 8 other lives to everyone from any caste or creed. Our vision is to build a nationally coordinated approach to educate and collect information about organ and tissue donation for transplantation in India.

About Organ Donation

Organ Donation

The process of Organ Donation comprises of transplanting healthy organs and tissues from one person to the other by the procedure of transplantation to restore the normal functioning of the body.

In India, each year about thousands of people die waiting for a suitable donor for transplant. By donating organs we give a second chance at life to those who are dying or leading a difficult life because of organ failure. To understand organ donation and finding a reason to donate let us understand that how humans die and how and in what circumstances we can be a donor.

Organ Transplantation

Organ Transplantation is a surgical procedure that involves the transplant of a healthy organ from a live/deceased donor into the body of the recipient (person with unhealthy organ/tissue).

The process consist of a dedicated criteria which ranges from the maintenance of waiting list of donors, finding a suitable donor and then arranging the transplant procedure. At Rotary Organ Donation Foundation we assess individual needs related to transplantation and then we provide information to help you discover your suitable donor.

Organ Donation in India

In Asia, India started its first Kidney transplant program in 1970’s. The success of the programme was remarkable but it came along with various controversies like “Kidney trade scandals” in the last two decades that made a bad impression where people found engaged in illegal commerce of organs for personal profits.

Despite of so many difficulties and allegations there were some Non Governmental Organisations, who were dedicatedly engaged in the promotion and conduction of organ transplant for the people who were genuinely seeking organs to live.

Why Donate

You will be surprised to know that an individual has the capacity to save upto 8 lives and can help up to 50 people through tissue and organ donation, and can improve the living conditions of many through tissue donation.

Its a boon to the mankind that Science have now given us the privilege to save the life of those people who die because of organ failure. With the procedure of transplantation it is now possible to save such lives. But the problem lies with the availability of suitable donors.

How to Become a Donor

Organ Donation is a legal process in India with no interference. The Indian government enacted the Transplantation of Human Organs Act (THOA), 1994, which allows organ donation.

It also legalized the concept of ‘brain death‘ which is a condition of irreversible and complete cessation of all brain functions. In such situation the person is kept on artificial support to fulfill the oxygen requirement of vital organs in the body and in that case, the patient’s organ can be transplanted to terminally ill patients.
