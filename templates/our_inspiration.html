PDG Ravi Choudhary

Organ donation is the most generous gift that any person can give to help someone live even after your death. It is appalling to see such a low level of organ donors in India. The need of the hour is to understand that one person can donate up to seven organs after death. Organ donation is a life-saving gift that we can all give to our fellow countrymen  and it costs us nothing. Let’s look at it as enabling another person live even after our death.

PDG Dr N. Subramanian

DG, RI Dist. 3011

The value of your work on organ and body donation is admirable. As a surgeon who is aware of the need for organ transplantation, the very small proportion of the large number requiring transplant actually managing it and the substantial change in quality of life after transplant, your contribution to this area is of immense value.

The national average of less than 10000 kidney transplants per year when compared to the need for 100000 is worrying. Similarly, a large proportion of corneal blindness can have their eyesight restored by corneal grafts commonly called keratoplasty.

Working at a hospital performing the largest number of liver transplantation in the south Asian region, I see the transformation TN the quality of life.

Cadaveric transplantation, which is ideal and constitutes a large percentage of transplants in the developed world, seems to be poorly received in our country, particularly in the Northern states.

Creating awareness and improving understanding of the medical and legal aspects of this process and it's inherent benefits in of paramount value.

I am only expressing an opinion, which I am sure is shared by many in the medical profession. Keep up the good work. We will be delighted to be of any assistance you may require.

DG Sanjiv Rai Mehra

(2020-21) RI District 3011

“Without the organ donor there is no story, no hope, no transplant. But when there is a organ donor, life springs from death, sorrow turns to hope and a terrible loss becomes a gift.”

A father recently donated the heart of his young daughter who had died in a terrible car accident to a critically ill patient suffering from a damaged heart. Thus giving life to another young person who would otherwise have died.

Little does one realize that the gift of life given to us by the lord above can be prolonged for many years beyond our life cycle by donating our organs. While we can donate six life saving organs and give hope of life to the donation recipients, but also donate other parts like eyes, tissues etc and thus benefit seventy five lives in all. WE give sight to two blind persons and open opportunities of colour in their lives.

As Rotarians it is our duty to spread the word about the immense benefits of organ donation and open opportunities of hope and joy for the otherwise hopeless.

Let us unite and take action to bring the joy of lasting change in the attitude of the public to donate their organs.”
